# 🐋 Public Dissemination Group Project Outline  
## Topic: **How Whales Eat — Teeth vs. Baleen**

---

## ⏱️ Time Breakdown (8 Minutes Total)

### **1. Hook (0:00–0:45)**
- Ask: *“How can the biggest animals on Earth eat tiny shrimp?”*
- Show contrast:
  - Whale chasing fish vs whale gulping water
- Goal: grab attention immediately

---

### **2. What Are Whales? (0:45–1:30)**
- Whales are:
  - Mammals (breathe air, warm-blooded)
  - Live in the ocean
- Two main types:
  - **Toothed whales**
  - **Baleen whales**

---

### **3. Toothed Whales (1:30–3:00)**
**Key Idea: They hunt individual prey**

- Examples: dolphins, orcas
- **Morphology (structure):**
  - Sharp teeth
  - Long snout
- **Function:**
  - Grab and hold prey (fish, squid)
- **Analogy:**
  - “Like using your hands to grab food”
- **Evolution idea:**
  - Adapted for active hunting

---

### **4. Baleen Whales (3:00–4:30)**
**Key Idea: They filter tiny prey from water**

- Examples: blue whale, humpback whale
- **Morphology:**
  - No teeth
  - Baleen plates (comb-like structures)
- **Function:**
  - Take in water → push it out → trap food
- **Analogy:**
  - “Like a strainer or filter”
- **Evolution idea:**
  - Adapted to eat massive amounts of tiny prey efficiently

---

### **5. Compare & Contrast (4:30–5:45)**

| Feature            | Toothed Whales        | Baleen Whales           |
|------------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Teeth            | Yes                  | No                       |
| Feeding Style    | Hunting              | Filtering                |
| Food             | Fish, squid          | Krill, plankton          |
| Strategy         | Fast, targeted       | Slow, massive intake     |

**Key takeaway:**  
Different structures → different jobs

---

### **6. Interactive Component (5:45–7:00)**

#### Option A (Zoom-friendly)
- Show images of whales
- Ask: *“Teeth or baleen?”*
- Let audience answer

#### Option B (Hands-on demo)
- Cup of water + small particles (paper bits)
- Use paper towel as a “filter”
- Demonstrates baleen feeding

---

### **7. Evolutionary Big Idea (7:00–7:40)**
- Whales didn’t all evolve the same way
- Different environments → different feeding strategies
- **Core idea:**
  - “Their bodies changed over time to match how they eat”

---

### **8. Closing (7:40–8:00)**
- Main takeaway:
  - “Some whales hunt with teeth, others filter with baleen—but both are perfectly adapted to survive.”
- Fun fact:
  - Blue whales are the largest animals ever

---
