# Public Dissemination Group Project Outline  
## Topic: How Whales Eat - Teeth vs. Baleen

---

## Time Breakdown (8 Minutes Total)

### 1. Hook (0:00–0:45)
- Ask: “How can the biggest animals on Earth eat tiny shrimp?”
- Show contrast:
  - Whale chasing fish vs whale gulping water
- Goal: grab attention immediately

---

### 2. What Are Whales? (0:45–1:30)
- Whales are:
  - Mammals (breathe air, warm-blooded)
  - Live in the ocean
- Two main types:
  - Toothed whales
  - Baleen whales

---

### 3. Toothed Whales (1:30–3:00)
Key idea: They hunt individual prey

- Example: orcas
- Morphology (structure):
  - Sharp teeth
  - Long snout
- Function:
  - Grab and hold prey (fish, squid)
- Analogy:
  - Like using your hands to grab food
- Evolution idea:
  - Adapted for active hunting

---

### 4. Baleen Whales (3:00–4:30)
Key idea: They filter tiny prey from water

- Examples: blue whale, humpback whale
- Morphology:
  - No teeth
  - Baleen plates (comb-like structures)
- Function:
  - Take in water → push it out → trap food
- Analogy:
  - Like a strainer or filter
- Evolution idea:
  - Adapted to eat large amounts of tiny prey efficiently

---

### 5. Compare & Contrast (4:30–5:45)

| Feature         | Toothed Whales     | Baleen Whales        |
|----------------|--------------------|----------------------|
| Teeth          | Yes                | No                   |
| Feeding Style  | Hunting            | Filtering            |
| Food           | Fish, squid        | Krill, plankton      |
| Strategy       | Fast, targeted     | Slow, bulk feeding   |

Key takeaway: different structures lead to different functions

---

### 6. Interactive Component (5:45–7:00)

Option A (Zoom-friendly)
- Show images of whales
- Ask: “Teeth or baleen?”
- Let audience answer

Option B (Simple demo)
- Cup of water + small paper pieces
- Use a paper towel as a filter
- Demonstrates baleen feeding

---

### 7. Evolutionary Big Idea (7:00–7:40)
- Whales did not all evolve the same feeding method
- Different environments led to different strategies
- Core idea:
  - Their anatomy changed over time to match how they eat

---

### 8. Closing (7:40–8:00)
- Main takeaway:
  - Some whales hunt with teeth, others filter with baleen, and both are well adapted to survive
- Optional fact:
  - Blue whales are the largest animals ever known

---

## Slide Structure (Minimal Text)

1. Title + hook question  
2. What is a whale  
3. Toothed whales  
4. Baleen whales  
5. Comparison table  
6. Interactive slide  
7. Evolution concept  
8. Conclusion  

---

## Group Role Split (3 People)

- Person 1: Intro + toothed whales  
- Person 2: Baleen whales + comparison  
- Person 3: Interactive + evolution + conclusion  

---

## Notes for Presentation

- Keep slides visual, not text-heavy  
- Use clear images to show structures  
- Speak in simple terms appropriate for middle school students  
- Stay within 8 minutes total  
- Make sure each person speaks evenly
